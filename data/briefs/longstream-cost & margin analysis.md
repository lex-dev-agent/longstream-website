# Cost & Margin Analysis — Longstream Distillery

## Per-Bottle Cost Breakdown (London Dry Gin, 700ml at 38% ABV)

### Current Setup (25L still, 9 bottles per batch, 14 fermentation vessels)

Based on Longstream's actual process: 21L sugar wash, double distillation (vodka run + gin run), spring water dilution. With 14 fermentation vessels and 2 batches ready per day, fermentation capacity supports up to 18 bottles/day (108/week). The bottleneck is still time and labour, not fermentation.

### Weekly Throughput Scenarios (Current 25L Still)

100 bottles/week is achievable with the current 25L still and fermentation setup. The question is whether the labour hours are sustainable.

## Channel Margin Analysis

At 100 bottles/week with 65L still + automation ($28.56 total cost per bottle):

| Channel | Revenue/Bottle | Margin/Bottle | Margin % |
|---|---|---|---|
| Direct (markets, website) | $69 | $40.44 | 59% |
| Retail (wholesale price) | $42 | $13.44 | 32% |
| Bars/restaurants (wholesale) | $35 | $6.44 | 18% |

Direct sales are critical for profitability. Even at scale, wholesale margins are thin because excise is a fixed cost that doesn't shrink with volume.

## Key Takeaways

1. **Excise is the dominant cost.** At $17.88/bottle, it is 65% of the cash cost. Nothing can be done about this.
2. **Fermentation is already solved.** 14 vessels with 2 batches/day ready provides ample capacity for 100+ bottles/week.
3. **Still time and labour are the real bottleneck.** Each batch needs ~8 hours of still time with ~9.5 hours of active labour.
4. **Automation transforms the labour equation.** Dropping active hours from 9.5 to ~2 per batch makes 2 batches/day sustainable as a side business.
5. **The 65L still reduces labour per bottle by 3x** (same hours, 3x output). It is the most impactful single investment for making the schedule manageable.
6. **Direct sales are essential** for margin. Wholesale/trade channels are viable at scale but margins are tight after excise.
7. **Break-even on capital investment is fast** if selling at RRP through direct channels.
