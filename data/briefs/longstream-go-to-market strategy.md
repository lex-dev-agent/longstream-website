# Go-To-Market Strategy — Longstream Distillery

## Positioning

**Longstream Distillery** — Small-batch New Zealand gin rooted in native botanicals, made from scratch in the Hutt Valley by the people who built the still.

### Brand Pillars
1. **Native botanicals** — Manuka, horopito, kawakawa. Not imported exotics, actual NZ bush ingredients.
2. **Made from scratch** — Full vertical: fermentation to bottle. Not bought-in neutral spirit.
3. **Engineer-built** — The automation story is genuinely interesting and differentiating. Two engineers building their own distillery, sensors and all. Lean into it.
4. **NZ spring water** — Spring water dilution is a quality marker in NZ gin.

### Positioning vs Competitors
- **Wildkiwi / Enceladus / Aurora / Ruin** (Upper Hutt, $60-80) — Local competitors at Brewtown and surrounds. Longstream differentiates on native botanical depth, the full-vertical process, and NZ-wide D2C focus rather than a local tasting-room play.
- **Reid + Reid** (Wairarapa, ~$70-85) — Also native botanicals. Strong brand. Longstream's engineering/automation story and rotating small batch specials are the differentiators.
- **Scapegrace** ($53-120) — National brand, premium positioning. Different tier; not a direct competitor at launch.

## Pricing Strategy

### Recommended Retail Price: $65-75 NZD (700ml)

**Rationale:**
- Excise duty alone is ~$18/bottle. Production costs will add another $8-15 (see Cost & Margin Analysis).
- The $65-75 range sits in the NZ craft gin sweet spot
- Competitive with Upper Hutt distilleries and regional brands
- Above entry-level craft ($45-55) where margins would be too thin after excise
- Small batch specials (Manuka & Chilli, Barrel-Rested, etc.) can push to $80-90

### Price Architecture
| Product | Recommended RRP | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| London Dry Gin (700ml) | $69 | Flagship, accessible entry point |
| Pure Vodka (700ml) | $59 | Lower perceived value for vodka, but high purity is the story |
| Limoncello (500ml) | $45 | Smaller bottle, lower ABV, gift-friendly |
| Small Batch Specials (700ml) | $79-89 | Limited runs, seasonal, higher margin |

### Channel Pricing
- **Direct to consumer** (website, farmers markets): full RRP
- **Wholesale to bars/restaurants**: 40-50% of RRP (~$28-35 per bottle)
- **Retail/bottle stores**: 30-40% margin to retailer (~$39-49 wholesale)

Direct sales are far more profitable. Prioritise them early.

## Sales Channels

### Phase 1: Launch (Months 1-6)
Focus on direct-to-consumer to maximise margin and build brand. NZ-wide from day one via online.

**1. Online D2C (longstream.nz)**
- Website already exists. Add e-commerce with nationwide shipping.
- Off-licence with Section 40 endorsement required for remote sales
- Two-stage age verification (site entry + checkout) mandatory
- Nationwide delivery via courier (NZ Post, CourierPost)
- Good for gift sets, subscription boxes, limited releases
- This is the primary channel for a NZ-wide strategy

**2. Wellington Region Farmers Markets**
- Upper Hutt Farmers Market (Sunday, at Brewtown)
- Harbourside Market (Wellington, Sunday)
- Thorndon Farmers Market
- Riverbank Market (Lower Hutt)
- Low overhead, direct customer interaction, instant feedback
- Great for testing small batch specials
- Need: off-licence, portable display, sampling licence for tastings

**3. Local Events and Pop-ups**
- Wellington craft beer/spirits festivals
- Beervana and similar events
- Food and wine events (Wairarapa, Martinborough)
- Special licence required per event

### Phase 2: Expansion (Months 6-12)
Once production is consistent and brand has traction.

**4. Bars and Restaurants (Wellington Region)**
- Start with cocktail-focused bars in Wellington CBD
- Approach restaurants with NZ-focused menus
- Self-distribute locally to start (avoids distributor margin)
- Story sells well in person: bring the engineer/maker angle to bar managers

**5. Local Bottle Stores**
- Independent/craft-focused stores first (not chains)
- Regional Wines, specialty liquor stores in Wellington
- Consignment or wholesale depending on relationship

### Phase 3: Scale (Year 2+)
When hitting 100 bottles/week consistently.

**6. National Distribution**
- Partner with a distributor: Hancocks, Beverage Brothers, or Wag & Co
- Opens access to bars, restaurants, and retail nationwide
- This is where NZ-wide positioning pays off vs being a "Wellington gin"

**7. National Online Retailers**
- The Liquor Cellar, Bevee.co.nz, BlackMarket.co.nz
- Wider reach without managing distribution yourself

**8. Cellar Door / Tasting Room (if premises allow)**
- Adds the tourism/experience angle. Upper Hutt's Brewtown shows this works locally.
- Requires on-licence
- Could be part of a move to dedicated premises (or a partnership with an existing venue)

## Market Sizing

### NZ-Wide (primary target)
- NZ spirit market projected to grow at 4.45% by 2027
- ~200 distilleries sharing the market
- NZ craft spirit market estimated at $50-100M annually
- Top craft brands (Scapegrace, Rogue Society) do $1-5M+ annually
- Realistic Year 1 target: $50-100K revenue (direct sales heavy)
- Year 2-3 target with distribution: $150-300K

### Wellington Region (local base)
- Population: ~540,000 (Greater Wellington)
- Strong craft/artisan culture, high disposable income demographics
- Already a craft spirits hub (Upper Hutt Brewtown, Wairarapa distilleries)
- Good for market testing and local brand building before scaling nationally

## Marketing

### Core Channels
- **Instagram** — primary platform for craft spirits. Behind-the-scenes distilling, botanical sourcing, the engineering/automation angle, market day photos.
- **Website/blog** — SEO for "NZ native botanical gin", "New Zealand craft gin", cocktail recipes
- **Email list** — built from markets and online sales. New release announcements, limited drops.
- **Word of mouth** — the most powerful channel for craft spirits. Quality product + good story = organic growth.

### Key Marketing Messages
- "Distilled from scratch in the Hutt Valley"
- "Native NZ botanicals: manuka, horopito, kawakawa"
- "Small batch. Engineer-built."
- Avoid startup-y language. Keep it grounded and real.

### Awards and PR
- Enter the NZ Small Batch Gin Awards early. Even a shortlist builds credibility.
- Local media angle: "Wellington engineers build automated craft distillery in Mangaroa" is a genuinely interesting story.
- Dish Magazine, NZ Herald food/drink section, Noted.co.nz, Capital Magazine (Wellington)
