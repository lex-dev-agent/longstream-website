# Regulatory Research — Longstream Distillery

## Overview

Selling spirits commercially in NZ requires navigating three regulatory layers: Customs/Excise (manufacturing and duty), liquor licensing (sales), and food safety/labelling (MPI). Home distilling for personal use is legal without a licence, but going commercial triggers all three.

## 1. Excise and Manufacturing Licence

### Licensed Manufacturing Area (LMA)
To manufacture spirits commercially, you need a Customs-Controlled Area (CCA) licence for your premises. This is managed by NZ Customs.

**Application process:**
1. Register on Trade Single Window (TSW)
2. Register your organisation in TSW, request "Excise Client" status
3. Register your premises as a CCA
4. Submit Fit and Proper Person Declaration (Form C12) with NZ Ministry of Justice criminal record (dated within 3 months)
5. Provide premises diagram (architectural plans)
6. Customs Excise Officer conducts a site visit
7. Once approved, register a declarant for monthly excise entries

**Key requirements:**
- Premises must meet security and operational standards
- At least one registered declarant for monthly excise returns
- The garage setup will need to meet CCA security standards (this may require upgrades)

### Excise Duty Rates (current)

| ABV Range | Rate (from 1 July 2024) | Rate (from 1 July 2025) |
|---|---|---|
| >14% to 23% vol | $67.220 per litre of alcohol | $68.915 per litre of alcohol |
| >23% vol | $67.220 per litre of alcohol | $68.915 per litre of alcohol |

Rates adjust annually on 1 July based on CPI movements.

**What this means for Longstream:**
- Gin at 38% ABV in a 700ml bottle contains 0.266L of pure alcohol
- Excise duty per bottle: 0.266 x $67.22 = **~$17.88 per bottle** (2024 rate)
- From July 2025: 0.266 x $68.915 = **~$18.33 per bottle**
- Plus Pae Ora (Healthy Futures) levy on top (annual adjustment, relatively small)

This is a significant per-bottle cost. At $17.88 excise alone, it sets a hard floor on retail pricing.

### Personal Use Exemption
Section 67 of the Customs and Excise Act 2018 allows manufacturing alcohol for personal use without a CCA licence or excise duty, provided it is not sold. Current garage production for personal consumption is legal.

## 2. Liquor Licence (Sale and Supply)

### Off-Licence
To sell spirits for consumption off-premises (online sales, farmers markets, retail distribution), you need an off-licence issued by your local District Licensing Committee (DLC).

**Requirements:**
- Application to Upper Hutt City Council DLC
- Certified Duty Manager with a current Manager's Certificate must be on duty during trading
- Police, Medical Officer of Health, and licensing inspector reports
- Premises suitability assessment

### On-Licence (if tasting room/cellar door planned)
If selling for consumption on-premises (tasting room, distillery tours), an on-licence is also required.

### 2025 Amendment Bill
The Sale and Supply of Alcohol Amendment Bill (April 2025) proposes changes that could benefit craft distilleries:
- Allows premises combining restaurant and shop to hold both on-licence and off-licence
- Amends revenue requirements for manufacturers with on-site retail to qualify for off-licence

Worth monitoring as this may simplify licensing for a distillery with a tasting room.

## 3. Food Safety (MPI)

### Food Control Plan
All food businesses, including distilleries, must have a food control plan under MPI. This covers:
- Production processes and safety controls
- Cleaning and sanitation
- Record keeping
- Engagement with MPI-recognised verifiers

### HACCP
The Brewers Guild has published a HACCP template for spirits production (v2.2, 2024). This provides a good starting framework for the food control plan.

## 4. Labelling Requirements

All labels must include:

| Requirement | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Product name** | e.g. "London Dry Gin" |
| **Alcohol content** | As % ABV (must be at least 37% ABV for gin) |
| **Standard drinks** | Number of standard drinks per container (to 1 decimal place if <10) |
| **Net contents** | Volume in mL or L |
| **Supplier information** | Physical NZ address |
| **Lot identification** | Batch/lot code |
| **Pregnancy warning** | Mandatory for >1.15% ABV |
| **Energy labelling** | **NEW from 13 August 2025** — energy content per serving and per 100ml, servings per package, serving size, standard drinks per serving |

### Gin-Specific Rules
- Must contain at least 37% ABV to be labelled as gin
- London Dry Gin has specific production requirements (no added flavourings after distillation, only water and sugar)
- Longstream's current 38% ABV meets the minimum

### Energy Labelling (August 2025)
This is a new requirement. Labels designed before August 2025 will need updating to include energy statements. Plan label design around this from the start.

## 5. Summary: Compliance Checklist

| Step | Responsible Party | Est. Timeline |
|---|---|---|
| CCA Licence (LMA) application via TSW | NZ Customs | 2-4 months |
| Fit and Proper Person check (C12 + criminal record) | NZ Customs / Ministry of Justice | 2-4 weeks |
| Premises security upgrades (if needed for CCA) | Longstream | Variable |
| Off-licence application to Upper Hutt City Council DLC | Upper Hutt DLC | 1-3 months |
| Manager's Certificate (Duty Manager) | Licensing authority | 4-6 weeks training + exam |
| Food Control Plan | MPI-recognised verifier | 1-2 months |
| Label design (compliant with all requirements) | Longstream / designer | 2-4 weeks |
| Register declarant for monthly excise returns | NZ Customs TSW | 1-2 weeks (after CCA approval) |

**Estimated total timeline to compliance: 4-6 months** (some steps can run in parallel)

## 6. Key Risks and Considerations

- **Excise duty is the single biggest regulatory cost** — ~$18/bottle before any production costs
- **Garage as CCA** — Customs will inspect and may require security upgrades. Purpose-built distillery spaces are more straightforward.
- **Manager's Certificate** — someone in the business needs formal certification. Requires completing a training course and exam.
- **Energy labelling deadline (Aug 2025)** — new requirement to factor into label design from the start
- **Monthly excise returns** — ongoing compliance obligation. Penalties for late or inaccurate filing.
- **Home insurance** — commercial production in a residential garage may void or complicate home insurance. Check with insurer.
- **Resource consent** — commercial activity in a residential zone may require resource consent from Upper Hutt City Council. Check district plan rules.
