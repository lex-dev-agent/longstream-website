Longstream Distillery — Business Overview

We're starting a craft spirit distilling business with a focus on gin. We've been making gin in our garage (30A breaker) and are now exploring bringing it to market. We've settled on the name Longstream Distillery and have a placeholder website up at longstream.nz.

Distilling Process

1. Fermentation — 7 days

A 24L sugar wash is prepared using granular sugar and a pack of yeast, then left to ferment in a bucket.

2. Vodka Run — ~4 hours

The wash is distilled in our 30L still to produce pure vodka:

  • Remove foreshots and a small amount of heads by estimated volume (manually watched).
  • Collect some heads into a small reservoir (manually watched).
  • Fill a larger reservoir with the remaining heads, all hearts, and some tails — this is our vodka.
  • Collect the final dregs of the tails into the same small reservoir as the heads (manually watched).
  • The small reservoir of heads and tails is recycled back into the still on the next vodka run.
  • Run the vodka through a long carbon filter to purify it.
  • Clean the still.

Output: ~4L at 80% ABV.

3. Gin Run — ~4 hours

  • Change the still head to the gin configuration.
  • Add the botanicals basket.
  • Redistil the vodka through the botanicals to produce a London Dry Gin.
  • Separate foreshots, heads, hearts (gin), and tails.
  • Clean the still.

4. Dilution & Bottling — ~1 hour

The gin is diluted by hand with spring water, measured to 41% ABV using a handheld refractometer, then bottled into recycled bottles.

Total per batch: ~7 days fermentation + ~9 hours active work → ~10 bottles of gin.

Products

Core Range

  • London Dry Gin — Our flagship. Botanical-forward, distilled from our own base spirit and diluted with spring water.
  • Pure Vodka — Carbon-filtered for exceptional purity. The vodka is very pure and stands on its own.
  • Limoncello — Made from our high-purity vodka base. Already a hit with friends and family.

Small Batch Specials

We like to experiment with limited runs of fun ideas like Manuka & Chilli Gin. These small batches are great for keeping people interested and engaged.

Scaling Up

Currently the process is very manual (e.g. physically lifting a bucket to pour the wash into the still). We're automation engineers with plenty of experience in sensors, motors, pumps, PLCs, Arduinos, web dashboards, etc. Our target is 100 bottles per week.

Planned Upgrades

  • Liquid sugar — Switch from granular to streamline wash preparation.
  • 2 × 60L stills — One dedicated to vodka, one to gin. The old 30L still moves to small-batch experiments.
  • Bottle standardisation — Decide between 700ml and 750ml.
  • Digital sensors — ABV sensors, temperature sensors, and depth sensors to replace manual monitoring.
  • Automated flow control — Pumps and solenoid diverter valves for an automated cut system, controlled via PLC/Arduino with a web dashboard.
